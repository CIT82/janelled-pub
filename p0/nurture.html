Nurture



Use positive reinforcement

Implement reward systems tailored to the child's interests, offering incentives for completing tasks or demonstrating positive behavior. This encourages desired actions and helps redirect attention from negative behaviors.

Create a sensory-friendly environment

Accommodate sensory processing issues by identifying and addressing triggers. This may include providing noise-canceling headphones, offering fidget toys, or designating quiet spaces for relaxation.

Focus on strengths and growth

Emphasize the child's unique abilities and celebrate their progress rather than fixating on specific developmental milestones. This approach fosters self-confidence and motivation while promoting continuous improvement at the child's own pace.

Teach and encourage self-advocacy skills

Help your child understand their unique strengths and challenges, and empower them to communicate their needs effectively. This includes teaching them how to express their feelings, ask for help when needed, and explain their accommodations to others. Preparing your child for what to expect in various situations and discussing how to handle potential challenges can boost their confidence and independence.
